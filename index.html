Help
How GymLog works
Installing the app

Open the link in your phone browser. Tap Share → Add to Home Screen on iPhone, or Install App on Android. GymLog will appear on your home screen and works offline.

Tip: when returning to GymLog mid-workout, use the app switcher (swipe up from the bottom or press the recent apps button) to return to your existing session. Tapping the home screen icon opens a fresh instance of the app.

If you do accidentally tap the home screen icon mid-workout, GymLog will detect your unfinished session and offer to Resume it when it reopens. Your data is saved automatically as you log.

Logging a workout

Tap Start Workout. Tap Add Exercise to add either strength training or cardio. For strength, log weight and reps. For cardio, log distance and time.

Tracking progress

Previous values auto-fill from your last session for each exercise. Tap ▸ Previous 3 sessions on any exercise to see your recent history. The Weekly Summary shows cardio output and sets per muscle group this week vs last week.

How your data is stored

Everything is stored directly on your device — nothing is sent to a server. GymLog uses an on-device database (IndexedDB) for workouts and localStorage for settings.

iPhone: tapping Clear History and Website Data in Safari settings will permanently erase all GymLog data. Android: only Clear Site Data affects GymLog — clearing the cache alone does not. Export a backup regularly to protect against accidental data loss.
